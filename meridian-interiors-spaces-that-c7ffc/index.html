The Studio

Celeste Varro —
Principal Designer

MERIDIAN was founded on a single belief: that the spaces we inhabit shape who we become. Celeste Varro established the studio in Charleston after a decade working alongside some of the most acclaimed interior practices in New York and London. She returned to the American South not as a retreat, but as a deliberate act — to build a practice rooted in place, craft, and the particular.

Every MERIDIAN project begins with deep listening. Celeste meets each client not with a portfolio of finishes, but with questions — about memory, ritual, light. The resulting interiors are never arbitrary. They are layered records of the people who live in them, achieved through architectural rigor, material honesty, and an unwillingness to settle for good when extraordinary is possible.

We deliberately limit our intake to twelve projects per year. This is not exclusivity for its own sake — it is the only way we know to do the work we were made to do.

Celeste Varro

How We Work

The MERIDIAN Process

Four deliberate phases, each one earning the next. We move slowly by design — because the alternative is a room you stop seeing within a year.

01

Discovery

An unhurried conversation about how you live, what you love, and what the space must accomplish. We listen before we sketch a single line.

02

Concept

We develop a singular creative vision — presented as a rich, tactile story board rather than a list of specifications. You should feel it before you see it.

03

Execution

Celeste oversees every contractor, artisan, and vendor personally. Standards are non-negotiable. We do not delegate what matters most.

04

Installation

The reveal is a ritual. Every object is placed, every surface addressed, every light level calibrated — before you walk through the door.

Frequently Asked

Common Questions

How far in advance should I reach out to begin a project?
We typically book projects six to twelve months in advance, depending on scope and timing. We encourage prospective clients to reach out as early as possible — even if your renovation isn't scheduled for some time. An initial conversation costs nothing, and early planning allows us to give your project the full creative attention it deserves.
What types of projects does MERIDIAN take on?
We work on high-end residential renovations — primary homes and vacation properties — boutique hospitality projects including hotels, inns, and restaurants, and full-service interior fit-outs for luxury residential buildings in collaboration with architects and developers. Each project is evaluated individually; we take on work where we believe we can contribute something genuinely extraordinary.
Do you work with clients outside of Charleston, SC?
Yes. While Charleston is our home and the origin of our aesthetic sensibility, we work with clients across the Southeast and beyond for the right project. Travel and accommodation costs for site visits are factored into our project proposals. We have completed projects in the Florida Keys, the North Carolina mountains, and New York.
What does your fee structure look like?
Our fees are structured on a project basis following an initial discovery meeting. We do not publish standard rates because every project has different requirements, timelines, and levels of involvement. What we can tell you is that our pricing reflects the depth of creative and logistical commitment we bring — and our client retention and referral rate speaks to the value delivered.
How involved will Celeste be in my project?
Celeste leads every project personally from discovery through installation. She does not delegate client relationships or creative direction to junior staff. This is precisely why we limit the number of projects we accept per year — so that every client has Celeste's genuine, sustained attention for the full duration of their engagement.

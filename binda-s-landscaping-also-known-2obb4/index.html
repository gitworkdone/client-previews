Frequently Asked
Questions

Common signs include standing water that remains 24–48 hours after rain, soggy or spongy lawn areas, erosion channels in your yard, water staining on your foundation, basement or crawlspace moisture, and soil that is constantly saturated near your home's perimeter. If you see any of these, contact us for a free site assessment.
Regrading is the process of reshaping the soil surface to create a positive slope away from your home and toward appropriate drainage outlets. It's needed when your yard has settled unevenly over time, was poorly graded during original construction, or when new drainage infrastructure requires resetting the surface grade to function correctly.
Most residential drainage installations are completed in 1–3 days. Larger regrading projects with extensive excavation may take 3–5 days. We will give you a precise project timeline before any work begins so you can plan accordingly.
